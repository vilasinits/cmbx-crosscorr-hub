CMBx cross-correlation introduction

The tSZ signal measures the line-of-sight integral of electron pressure, while Euclid probes the matter field through galaxy clustering and weak lensing. Cross-correlating them connects thermodynamics and structure formation in one framework. In practice, the workflow runs from data products and map-level spectra, through halo-model theory, to covariance construction and Bayesian inference.

Maps and catalogs

Planck or ACT Compton-$y$ maps, CMB lensing maps, and Euclid galaxy / lensing samples define the measured observables.

→

Theory layer

Halo profiles, baryonic suppression, Limber projection, and noise / covariance models produce the prediction for $C_\ell$ and related statistics.

→

Inference layer

Likelihood assembly, MCMC diagnostics, and posterior analysis convert the measured cross-correlations into cosmological and astrophysical constraints.

Go to CLOE for interface notes and integration-facing documentation that sits between pipeline code and likelihood usage.
